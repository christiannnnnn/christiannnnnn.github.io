The Creation of Axie Infinity

Axie Infinity is a non-fungible token-based online video game developed by Vietnamese studio named Sky Mavis, known for its in-game economy which uses Ethereum-based cryptocurrencies. Players of Axie Infinity collect and mint NFTs which represent axoloti-inspired digital pets known as Axies. These creatures can be bred and battled with each other within the game.

Sky Mavis team

Axie Infinity's development were led by the CEO and co-founder of Sky Mavis, Nguyen Thanh Trung, with Tu Doan, Aleksander Larsen, Jeffrey Zirlin, and Andy Ho. Before making his own game, he used to spent his money on a game called Cryptokitties, he then combined elements of Cryptokitties with the gameplay from Pokemon series or Neopets. In October 2018, the team released the first battle system of Axie Infinity. Development of the real-time card battle system and application commenced in March 2019 and an alpha was released in December 2019.

At September 2021, Axie Infinity became popular due to the players who are from Philippines and were earning big with the game.

    Axie Infinity is built on the Ronin Network, an Ethereum-linked sidechain dveloped by Sky Mavis itself. The game's token, SLP, crashed in February 2022 amid a wider NFT and cryptocurrency crash which made it lost over 99% of its peak value. In March 2022, hackers compromised the Ronin Network and stole approximately US$620 million worth of cryptocurrency from the project.
